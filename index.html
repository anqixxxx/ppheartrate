Your current heart rate is above the healthy range.

Why a High Heart Rate is Concerning:

An elevated heart rate can indicate stress, dehydration, or underlying health issues. It's important to address this promptly to avoid potential health risks.

Immediate Steps to Take:

1. Take deep breaths and try to relax. Sit or lie down if possible.

2. Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

3. Avoid stimulants like caffeine and nicotine.

4. If you experience chest pain, dizziness, or shortness of breath, seek medical attention immediately.

Long-Term Tips for Maintaining a Healthy Heart Rate:

1. Engage in regular physical activities like walking, jogging, or cycling.

2. Maintain a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains.

3. Practice stress-relieving activities such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises.

4. Monitor your heart rate regularly to track your progress.

Why a Low Heart Rate is Concerning:

A low heart rate can indicate good physical fitness in athletes, but it can also suggest an issue with the heart's electrical system, particularly in non-athletes. It's important to address this to avoid potential health risks.

Immediate Steps to Take:

1. Take deep breaths and try to relax. Sit or lie down if possible.

2. Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

3. If you experience dizziness, fatigue, or fainting, seek medical attention immediately.

Long-Term Tips for Maintaining a Healthy Heart Rate:

1. Engage in regular physical activities like walking, jogging, or cycling.

2. Maintain a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains.

3. Practice stress-relieving activities such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises.

4. Monitor your heart rate regularly to track your progress.
